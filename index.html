About Me

I'm Kangyou Yu, a first year PhD student at The University of Melbourne, co-supervised by Adelaide Genay and Jorge Goncalves. I obtained a Master degree in Computer Science at UCSB, where I was pursuing my PhD program in Four Eyes Lab under the supervision of Tobias Höllerer at UCSB untill Dec. 2024. Before that, I received my Bachelor degree in Information and Computing Science from Xi’an Jiaotong-Liverpool University (Suzhou, China) and University of Liverpool (Liverpool, United Kingdom) in July 2022 and worked as an undergraduate research assistant at X-CHI Lab, supervised by Hai-Ning Liang and Wenge Xu .

I also worked as a research intern in the APEX Group, supervised by Mingming Fan (The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou), China). I also worked as a remote research intern in the VVISE Lab, supervised by Wolfgang Stuerzlinger (Simon Fraser University, Canada) and Anil Ufuk Batmaz (now at Concordia University, Canada).

My research interest lies on Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and AR/VR/MR and focus on input modalities in Mixed Reality and making intelligent system/devices understand human behaviors/capabilities, creating interactive system which can be adaptive to changing contexts in AR/VR/MR.
